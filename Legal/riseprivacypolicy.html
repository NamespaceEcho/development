RISE PRIVACY POLICY

Last Updated: June 17, 2026.

This Privacy Policy explains how <Namespace> Development (“us”, “we”, or “our”) handles data when playing Rise and cross-communicating with Unity Gaming Services (“UGS”). The core of this policy is simple: Your personal data is your business, not ours!

1. Information Not Collected

Rise is designed to operate without tracking you. It does not collect, share, or process any identifying or personal data about you or your device. This includes:

  • Personal Information: Rise will not ask for or collect names, email addresses, or account passwords.
  • Geolocation Data: Rise will not track your physical location, GPS data, or IP-based data.
  • Financial Data: Rise has no in-app purchases or pay-to-win mechanics. Therefore, Rise will never ask for any payment or financial information.

2. Information Collected

The only data that is collected from Rise to our UGS servers is your in-game score, which is used strictly to populate the global leaderboards.

3. Leaderboard Profiles & Anonymity

3.1 Leaderboard Profiles

To keep your data private and allow you to participate in the leaderboards, Rise generates an encrypted, reusable session token that only UGS can recognize. This session token is generated when you first connect to UGS.

  • Your profile allows Rise to connect to the leaderboards without a username and password combo.
  • Your profile is separated from hardware; it does not contain device fingerprints, MAC addresses, or identifiers.
  • Your profile cannot be traced back to your physical machine or real-world identity.

3.2 Offline Gameplay

Accessing the leaderboards requires having a network or data connection. However, not being able to connect will not affect gameplay. Rise is designed so that it does not rely on any streaming assets or network connections to get the full experience while offline.

Your game data is saved both locally and on the leaderboards. You do not have to connect to UGS to keep your data current.

4. Local Backup Files & Security

4.1 Backups

Because leaderboard profiles are anonymous, transferring your progress and continuing your session on another machine can not be done with a typical login. Instead, you can export a backup file containing both your progress and session key. The resulting file location depends on your operating system.

  • Windows: %userprofile%\AppData\LocalLow\NamespaceDevelopment\Rise
  • MacOS: ~/Library/Application Support/unity.NamespaceDevelopment.Rise

The file generated is named (“rise_progress.bkup”).

4.2 Security Risks

Because your session key is stored in the backup file, this poses a possible security risk for your profile. As such, we ask that you please act responsibly when exporting and keep this file secure! If you share this file with anyone, they can connect to your leaderboard profile and overwrite your scores.

4.3 Recovery & Loss

Once a profile is breached, that profile cannot be recovered and must be banned. However, it is possible to recover the data from the profile. However, the process is extensive and requires:

  • Having a recent local backup
  • Performing a fresh installation of Rise to generate a new profile
  • Coordinating with support to splice your data into your new profile.

We strongly urge you to keep your backup files completely private and back up your progress only if you’re continuing your session on another machine!

5. Children’s Privacy

Rise is a game intended for all ages. However, we do not knowingly collect or maintain personal information from children under the age of 13. For players under the age of 13, it’s recommended to ask a parent or guardian to approve the purchase of the game and to have them read the privacy policy to understand the full scope of how data is collected and managed.

To any parents reading this policy, let us reassure you that your child’s safety and privacy are cornerstones of <Namespace> Development and Rise.

6. User Data Rights

Because Rise does not collect any personal identifying information, there’s no personal data for users to access or delete. However, you still reserve the right to have your leaderboard data reset or your profile erased.
